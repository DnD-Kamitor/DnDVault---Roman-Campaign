# Introduction

## Campaign Overview

*The Shadow of Mars* is a 5-session campaign set in a fantastical Roman world where the gods are real, auguries matter, and destiny can be changed—or embraced.

The party plays as members of a military unit stationed at a remote frontier fort. When dark omens appear and a powerful artifact is unearthed, they must decide whether to serve Rome, the gods, or their own ambitions.

**Levels:** Characters start at level 3 and advance to level 7 by the campaign's end.

**Themes:** Duty vs. ambition, fate vs. free will, loyalty vs. survival, divine intervention

**Setting:** The campaign takes place along Rome's Germanic frontier in 175 AD, in an alternate world where D&D magic exists alongside Roman military discipline and pantheon worship.

## Key NPCs

- **Legate Marcus Aurelius Corvinus** – Commander of the fort, ambitious and ruthless
- **Augur Cassia Liviana** – The fort's seer, troubled by dark visions
- **Centurion Titus Varro** – Veteran soldier, loyal but pragmatic
- **Tribune Lucius Valerius Maximus** – A mysterious figure from Rome with a hidden agenda
- **Vercingetorix the Red** – Germanic chieftain and reluctant ally
- **Senator Gaius Cassius Brutus** – Roman aristocrat with revolutionary ambitions

## NPC OGAS Framework (Guy Sclanders Method)

### Legate Marcus Aurelius Corvinus

**Objective:** Secure a military triumph and consulship in Rome
**Goal:** Possess the Spear of Mars as proof of his frontier victory
**Agenda:** Use the artifact to gain Senate favor and advancement
**Secret:** He's drowning in gambling debts to a powerful Senator (Brutus) who now owns him

**Personality:** Blunt, aggressive, views everything through military lens
**Speaks like:** A drill sergeant - short, commanding sentences
**Reacts to opposition:** With anger first, calculation second
**If threatened:** Will sacrifice subordinates to save himself

### Augur Cassia Liviana

**Objective:** Prevent divine catastrophe and protect Rome from Mars' wrath
**Goal:** Destroy the Spear of Mars before it corrupts anyone else
**Agenda:** Guide the party toward making the right choice without forcing them
**Secret:** She's Mars' daughter (demigod) and can feel the spear calling to her bloodline

**Personality:** Quiet, intense, speaks in riddles when nervous
**Speaks like:** An oracle - cryptic, layered meanings, pauses between thoughts
**Reacts to opposition:** With sadness rather than anger; tries to show visions
**If threatened:** Will sacrifice herself to stop the spear

### Centurion Titus Varro

**Objective:** Survive his final year of service and retire with honor
**Goal:** Keep his soldiers alive and maintain order at the fort
**Agenda:** Support whoever seems most competent—party or commander
**Secret:** He knows the Legate is corrupt but can't prove it; 20 years of service make him complicit

**Personality:** Pragmatic, weary, dark humor to cope with violence
**Speaks like:** A veteran NCO - profane, direct, occasional wisdom
**Reacts to opposition:** With exhausted reason; "I've seen this before"
**If threatened:** Will choose his men over his commander every time

### Tribune Lucius Valerius Maximus

**Objective:** Earn respect as a "real Roman" despite his aristocratic birth
**Goal:** Complete his secret mission from Senator Brutus (destroy the spear)
**Agenda:** Manipulate the party into destroying it so he gets credit
**Secret:** He's Senator Brutus' illegitimate son, desperate to prove his worth

**Personality:** Overconfident, theatrical, masks insecurity with arrogance
**Speaks like:** An aristocrat - flowery language, quotes philosophers, condescending
**Reacts to opposition:** With wounded pride; becomes petty and vindictive
**If threatened:** Will betray anyone to survive; coward beneath the bravado

### Vercingetorix the Red

**Objective:** Protect his people from Roman expansion
**Goal:** Recover the Spear of Mars (killed his grandfather, tribe's sacred enemy)
**Agenda:** Use the party to get past Roman defenses, then take the spear by force or alliance
**Secret:** He's dying from a wasting disease; this is his last campaign; wants his death to mean something

**Personality:** Noble warrior, tired of war, respects strength and honesty
**Speaks like:** Poetic warrior - comparisons to nature, warrior's code
**Reacts to opposition:** Tests strength first, respects those who stand firm
**If threatened:** Will fight to the death but prefers honorable single combat

### Senator Gaius Cassius Brutus

**Objective:** "Restore the Republic" (overthrow the Emperor, install himself as First Citizen)
**Goal:** Acquire the Spear of Mars to assassinate the Emperor during his Triumph
**Agenda:** Manipulate everyone as pawns; eliminate witnesses once spear is secured
**Secret:** He's already made pacts with three other gods (Jupiter, Pluto, Bellona) promising them Mars' power if they support his coup

**Personality:** Charismatic sociopath, genuinely believes he's Rome's savior
**Speaks like:** Statesman - rhetorical questions, appeals to Roman values, very persuasive
**Reacts to opposition:** With charm first, then legal/political pressure, then assassination
**If threatened:** Flees and regroups; will burn Rome before admitting failure

### Mars (God of War)

**Objective:** Reclaim his honor after mortals stole and destroyed his divine weapon
**Goal:** Make the party understand the consequences of defying a god
**Agenda:** Test their worth—if strong, make them his champions; if weak, destroy them
**Secret:** He's secretly proud they destroyed the corrupted spear; it had become a blight on his name

**Personality:** Wrathful but honorable, respects courage above all
**Speaks like:** God of war - imperious, archaic Latin mixed with English, SHORT and LOUD
**Reacts to opposition:** With fury but also respect; violence is prayer to him
**If threatened:** Cannot truly die; will curse them across lifetimes if dishonored

## Using OGAS in Play

**When NPCs appear:**
1. Start with their **Goal** - what they want THIS SESSION
2. Use their **Agenda** - HOW they'll try to get it
3. Reveal **Objective** through multiple sessions
4. Drop **Secret** hints for clever players to discover

**Example: When party meets Legate (Session 1)**
- Goal: Get them to retrieve spear → Direct orders
- Agenda: Establish authority → Threatens court-martial if they refuse
- Objective leak: "Securing this artifact will echo all the way to Rome"
- Secret hint: Cassia whispers, "The Legate is not his own master"

**If party goes off-script:** NPCs pursue their OGAS regardless of party actions. This makes world feel reactive and alive.

## The Central Mystery

A cursed artifact called the *Spear of Mars* has been unearthed near the fort. Legend says it once belonged to the war god himself and was used to kill a divine being. Now it whispers to those who touch it, promising power and glory while slowly corrupting their souls.

The party must decide: destroy it, claim it, or deliver it to Rome?

# Chapter 1: Blood and Omens

**Applying Guy Sclanders' Methodology** *(Based on [The Complete Guide to Creating Epic Campaigns](https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Complete_Guide_to_Creating_Epic_Camp.html?id=0XFNvgEACAAJ) and [How to be a Great Game Master](https://www.greatgamemaster.com/dm/about-us/))*

## Session Overview

The party arrives at Fort Vindolanda on the Germanic frontier. Strange omens plague the camp, and the party is sent to investigate a nearby excavation where something ancient has been unearthed.

**Level:** 3 → Advance to 4 at session end
**Duration:** 3-4 hours
**Key Goal:** Investigate the excavation site and decide the spear's fate
**Theme:** Duty vs. Free Will (first exploration)

### Guy Sclanders' Principles Applied

**Pressure Cooker Design:**
- Time pressure: The spear is corrupting the fort NOW
- Incomplete information: Who can be trusted?
- Escalating stakes: Personal → Fort → Rome → Divine

**OGAS Integration:** Legate, Augur, and Centurion all have clear goals THIS SESSION

**Meaningful Choices:** No "correct" answer; every choice has consequences

## Pre-Session Preparation

### Props & Handouts (Physical Immersion)

**Essential Props:**
1. **Wax Tablet** - Write the Legate's orders in Latin on a small board
2. **Red Dice** - For corruption tracking (give corrupted players red d6s)
3. **Omen Cards** - Index cards with Latin phrases describing bad omens
4. **Spear Token** - Black painted dowel to represent the spear physically

**Handout 1: Legate's Orders (Latin/English)**
```
MANDATUM LEGATI MARCI AURELII CORVINI

Ad milites electos:
Descendite in ruinas. Recuperate telum divinum.
Nemo tangat telum nisi ego permitto.

(To the chosen soldiers:
Descend into the ruins. Recover the divine weapon.
No one shall touch the weapon unless I permit it.)

In nomine Senatus Populique Romani.
```

**Handout 2: Gaulish Runes Translation**
```
GAULISH/CELTIC INSCRIPTION:
"Atir nertos, atir bitus, atir maros"
(Father of strength, father of life, father of death)

"Ro·bið·su in gaissos·mo dîvon-macon"
(Here lies the spear, god-slayer)

TRANSLATION:
Here lies the God-Killer, the Spear of Broken Oaths,
sealed by the blood of heroes. May it never taste sunlight again.
Forged in vengeance. Quenched in divine blood.
The son of Mars fell here. His father's wrath lingers still.
```

**Handout 3: Omens Observed (Give to players as prop)**
- All camp dogs died at midnight (no visible cause)
- Sacrificial sheep had black livers (sign of divine curse)
- Ravens fly backwards over the principia
- Springs run red with iron-tainted water
- Sentries report shadows moving without bodies
- Legionaries hear distant war-horns (no enemy visible)

## Scene 0: Cold Open (5 minutes)
**Purpose:** Hook players immediately with visceral horror (Guy Sclanders: "Strong Opening")

**Start in media res - No preamble:**

**Read aloud (speak slowly, with dread):**
> *Three nights ago.*
>
> You dream. All of you, the same dream. You stand in darkness, ankle-deep in blood that doesn't ripple. In the distance, a spear stands upright in the earth, glowing like a dying star. When you try to look away, you can't. When you try to wake, you can't.
>
> A voice—old, vast, furious—speaks a single word in a language you shouldn't understand, but do:
>
> **"MEUM." (MINE.)**
>
> You wake. Your tent reeks of copper and smoke. Outside, the camp dogs are howling.
>
> By dawn, every dog in Fort Vindolanda is dead.

**DM Direction:** Don't answer questions yet. Cut to black, then:

**Read aloud:**
> *Now.*
>
> The principia. The Legate's headquarters. The air is thick with incense, but underneath it, you smell rot. Around the table: Legate Corvinus, eyes bloodshot and intense. Augur Cassia, pale as death, clutching a bloodstained cloth. Centurion Varro, one hand on his gladius, looking like he hasn't slept.
>
> The Legate speaks. His voice is iron scraping stone:
>
> "You've been chosen. Not for honor. For survival. Something is beneath this fort. Something that killed sixty dogs in one night. Something that made the augurs vomit blood during morning sacrifice. We've lost four men already—two to madness, two to each other's blades."
>
> He unrolls a rough sketch of underground ruins.
>
> "You're going down there. You're bringing it up. You're not asking questions."
>
> Across the table, the Augur whispers: "*Mars aversus est.* The god of war has turned his face from us."

**Pressure Cooker Element:** Players don't control the situation yet—they're already deep in crisis

## Scene 1: Meeting the Legate

The party is summoned to meet **Legate Marcus Aurelius Corvinus** in the principia. He's a hard man in his forties, scarred and ambitious.

**Legate's Briefing:**
- Three days ago, a work crew digging a new well discovered ancient ruins beneath the fort
- They unearthed an iron spear covered in strange runes
- That night, all the camp dogs died
- The next day, the augurs found corrupted omens: black livers, reversed bird flights
- One worker touched the spear and went mad, killing two companions before being subdued
- The Legate wants the party to go down into the ruins, secure the artifact, and bring it to him

**Complications:**
- **Augur Cassia Liviana** interrupts, warning that the omens forbid disturbing the spear
- **Centurion Varro** suggests destroying it immediately
- The Legate overrules them both—Rome must possess this weapon

**Decision Point:** Will the party obey orders or heed the augur's warning?

## Scene 2: Descent into the Ruins

The excavation site is a crude shaft dug through Roman stonework into older Germanic structures. The air grows cold as the party descends.

**Exploration:**
- Ancient Germanic temple dedicated to a forgotten war god
- Walls covered in bronze age runes (DC 15 History or Religion to read)
- Runes tell a story: *"Here lies the God-Killer, the Spear of Broken Oaths, sealed by the blood of heroes. May it never taste sunlight again."*
- Three chambers:
  1. **Hall of Shields** – Dozens of ancient shields line the walls, some bearing symbols of forgotten tribes
  2. **Chamber of Chains** – Iron chains hang from ceiling, sized for something enormous
  3. **The Vault** – Central chamber where the spear was found, now guarded by **animated armor** (2x) possessed by vengeful spirits

**Combat Encounter:** 2 Animated Armor (CR 1 each)
- The spirits scream: "DO NOT TAKE THE SPEAR!"
- If defeated: They dissolve, whispering "You have doomed us all"

## Scene 3: The Spear

The **Spear of Mars** rests on a stone altar, surrounded by broken chains. It's eight feet long, made of black iron, covered in glowing red runes.

**Mechanics:**
- Anyone who touches it must make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw
- **Failure:** Visions of glory and conquest flood their mind. They gain 1 level of corruption (track this)
- **Success:** They resist but feel its pull

**Properties (if attuned):**
- +2 magic weapon
- Deals an extra 1d8 necrotic damage
- Disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws
- User becomes increasingly aggressive and paranoid

**Roleplaying:** The spear whispers promises:
- "You could be a hero"
- "Rome will kneel before you"
- "Your enemies will break like wheat"

## Scene 4: Return to the Surface

As the party emerges, they find the fort in chaos:
- The mad worker has escaped his bonds
- He's killed three guards and is rampaging through the camp
- He's screaming: "THE SPEAR CALLS FOR BLOOD!"

**Combat Encounter:** Corrupted Worker (use **Berserker** stat block, CR 2)
- If the party has the spear, he focuses on whoever carries it
- Dies screaming: "Mars...demands...sacrifice..."

## Conclusion

The Legate demands the spear. The Augur begs them to destroy it. Centurion Varro looks to the party for guidance.

**Key Questions:**
- Do they give the spear to the Legate?
- Do they try to destroy it?
- Does anyone attune to it?

**Advance to Level 4**

## DM Notes

### Roman Military Ranks (for reference)
- **Legate** – Commander of the legion (like a general)
- **Tribune** – Staff officer from Rome (aristocrat)
- **Centurion** – Company commander (60-80 men)
- **Optio** – Second-in-command to centurion
- **Legionary** – Standard soldier

### Corruption Mechanic
Track corruption levels (0-5) for any character who touches or attunes to the spear:
- **Level 1:** Disturbing dreams of conquest
- **Level 2:** Disadvantage on Wisdom saves
- **Level 3:** Must succeed DC 12 Wisdom save to retreat from combat
- **Level 4:** Must succeed DC 14 Wisdom save to avoid attacking allies when bloodied
- **Level 5:** Fully corrupted, becomes NPC under DM control

### Optional Encounters
- **Scouts' Report:** Germanic tribe activity increased in the forest
- **Camp Tensions:** Legionaries are terrified, some talk of desertion
- **The Augur's Warning:** Private conversation revealing that the spear belongs to Mars and was used in a divine war
# Chapter 2: The Tribune's Gambit

## Session Overview
A Tribune arrives from Rome with secret orders regarding the spear. Meanwhile, Germanic raiders attack the fort, and the party must defend it while uncovering the Tribune's true agenda.

**Level:** 4 → Advance to 5 at session end
**Key Goal:** Defend the fort and discover the Tribune's mission

## Opening Scene: The Tribune Arrives

Three days after the spear's discovery, a Tribune arrives from Rome with an elite escort of Praetorian Guards. He's young, polished, and dangerously intelligent.

**Read aloud:**
> The gates open to admit a small column of riders in gleaming armor. At their head rides a man too clean for the frontier—purple-trimmed toga, oiled hair, rings on every finger. He surveys the fort like a merchant appraising damaged goods. Behind him, six Praetorians sit their horses like statues of iron and menace.

**Tribune Lucius Valerius Maximus** introduces himself with imperial authority:
- He carries sealed orders from the Senate
- The spear is to be delivered to Rome immediately
- The Legate is relieved of command pending investigation
- The party is promoted to "Special Envoys of the Senate" (higher authority than the Legate)
- They will escort the Tribune and the spear to Rome

**Complications:**
- The Legate is furious but powerless
- The Augur warns this is exactly what Mars wants—the spear in Rome
- Centurion Varro quietly tells the party: "That Tribune is lying about something"

## Scene 1: Investigation & Tensions

The party has time to investigate before departure. Options:

**Talk to the Augur (Cassia Liviana):**
- She's had visions: the spear in Rome, the Capitoline Hill running with blood
- She reveals the spear's true history: it was used by a mortal hero to kill a minor god (Mars' son)
- Mars cursed it so that whoever wields it will be consumed by war
- The Tribune knows this—he plans to use it to assassinate someone in Rome

**Talk to Centurion Varro:**
- He's served 20 years and knows political games
- "That Tribune didn't come here for the spear—he came here to disappear the spear"
- He suspects the Tribune plans to fake an attack and blame the party
- Offers to help if things go wrong

**Search the Tribune's Quarters (DC 16 Investigation):**
- Find a letter (encoded, DC 14 Intelligence to decipher)
- The letter is from a Senator ordering the Tribune to "ensure the artifact never reaches Rome"
- The Tribune is supposed to destroy it—or those carrying it

## Scene 2: The Raid

That night, Germanic raiders attack the fort in force. This is coordinated with the Tribune's arrival (suspicious timing).

**Battle Map:** Fort under siege
- 20+ raiders (use **Tribal Warrior** stat blocks in waves)
- 2 **Berserkers** (CR 2 each)
- 1 **Germanic Chieftain** - **Vercingetorix the Red** (use **Gladiator** stat block, CR 5)

**Combat Phases:**

**Phase 1: The Walls (2 rounds)**
- Raiders swarm the palisade with ladders
- Party can man ballistae (2d10 piercing, DC 12 Dex to aim)
- Archers provide supporting fire

**Phase 2: Breach (3 rounds)**
- Raiders break through the gate with a battering ram
- Melee combat in the fort courtyard
- Centurion Varro fights alongside the party

**Phase 3: The Headquarters (2 rounds)**
- Vercingetorix and berserkers push toward where the spear is kept
- The Tribune and his Praetorians mysteriously don't help
- Climactic battle to defend the principia

## Scene 3: The Truth Revealed

After the battle, as the party catches their breath:

**The Tribune's Move:**
- He accuses the party of being traitors who let the raiders in
- Claims they were trying to steal the spear
- Orders his Praetorians to arrest them
- The Legate, humiliated and angry, supports this

**Centurion Varro's Intervention:**
- He steps forward: "The Tribune is the traitor!"
- Reveals what he saw: the Tribune signaling the raiders from the walls
- The Praetorians hesitate—they respect Varro as a veteran

**Decision Point:**
The party must choose:
1. **Fight** – Combat with Tribune + 6 Praetorians (deadly)
2. **Diplomacy** – Convince the Praetorians (DC 16 Persuasion with evidence)
3. **Flee** – Escape with the spear into the Germanic forest
4. **Surrender** – Give up the spear to see what happens

## Conclusion Options

**If they expose the Tribune:**
- He confesses under pressure: he was ordered to ensure the spear never reached Rome
- A powerful Senator fears what it could do in the wrong hands
- He offers them a deal: help him destroy it, and they'll all walk away free
- The Augur supports destroying it

**If they flee:**
- They're now fugitives from Rome with a cursed spear
- Vercingetorix intercepts them in the forest
- He offers alliance: "My enemy's enemy is my friend"

**If they fight:**
- Chaotic battle, possible deaths
- They must flee afterward
- Now hunted by both Rome and the Germanic tribes

**Advance to Level 5**

## DM Notes

### Vercingetorix the Red (Germanic Chieftain)
- **Motivation:** He's not evil—he's defending his homeland from Roman expansion
- Can become an unlikely ally if the party treats him with respect
- Has his own history with the spear: it killed his grandfather

### The Tribune's Secret
Lucius Valerius is caught between loyalty to Rome and orders from a corrupt Senator. He's not wholly evil—he genuinely believes destroying the spear is best for Rome. This creates moral complexity.

### Pacing
This session should feel tense and chaotic. The raid happens fast, and the party must make quick decisions with incomplete information—classic Guy Sclanders "pressure cooker" design.
# Chapter 3: Through the Dark Forest

## Session Overview

The party must journey through the Teutoburg Forest—either fleeing Rome or on a secret mission to destroy the spear. They face natural dangers, Germanic tribes, and the spear's corrupting influence.

**Level:** 5 → Advance to 6 at session end
**Key Goal:** Reach the sacred grove where the spear can be destroyed

## Opening: The Choice is Made

Based on Session 2's ending, the party now has a clear goal:
- **If allied with Tribune:** Secret mission to destroy the spear at an ancient grove
- **If fugitives:** Hunted by Rome, seeking refuge with Vercingetorix
- **If holding spear:** Fighting its corrupting whispers every day

**Read aloud:**
> The forest swallows you whole. Ancient oaks rise like cathedral columns, their canopy so thick that even at midday, twilight reigns. The Romans call this the Dark Forest—the place where legions disappear. Your guide (Tribune/Vercingetorix/Augur) moves carefully, watching the shadows. Behind you, somewhere in the green gloom, pursuers gather.

## Scene 1: The Haunted Grove

The party camps in a grove that feels wrong—too quiet, too still.

**That night:**
- Anyone attuned to the spear has vivid nightmares (Wisdom save DC 15 or gain exhaustion)
- Visions of ancient battles, blood-soaked altars, screaming gods
- The spear whispers: "You could end this. Take me to Rome. Rule the world."

**Corruption Check:**
- Anyone who touched the spear must make a Wisdom save (DC 14)
- Failure: Corruption level increases by 1
- At corruption level 3+, they begin arguing for keeping the spear

**Morning Discovery:**
- Three legionaries are found dead, throats cut
- No tracks, no signs of struggle
- One has carved into a tree: "MARS SEES ALL"

**Investigation (DC 14 Survival):**
- The killer is among the party's NPCs
- One of them (your choice) is possessed by Mars' influence through the spear
- They'll try to kill whoever plans to destroy it

## Scene 2: The Germanic Ambush

Germanic scouts have tracked the party. Depending on their relationship with Vercingetorix:

**If Allied with Vercingetorix:**
- His scouts find them, offer safe passage
- But they demand a toll: information about Roman troop movements
- Moral dilemma: betray Rome or fight your allies?

**If Not Allied:**
- Ambush by 12 **Tribal Warriors** + 2 **Scouts** (CR 1/2 each)
- Combat in dense forest (difficult terrain, limited visibility)
- Can be ended via diplomacy if they drop weapons and negotiate

**Key NPC:** **Thusnelda**, a Germanic druid
- She knows of the spear: "The God-Killer walks again"
- Offers to guide them to the sacred grove where it can be unmade
- Warns: "The grove demands a sacrifice—blood for blood, life for death"

## Scene 3: The River Crossing

The party must cross a swollen river to reach the sacred grove.

**Challenges:**
- River is 100 feet wide, fast-flowing (DC 14 Athletics to swim)
- Fallen tree serves as a bridge (DC 12 Acrobatics to cross safely)
- Rope can be strung across with teamwork

**Complication: The Hunters Arrive**

As they cross, Roman pursuers catch up:
- 8 **Legionaries** (use **Guard** stat blocks)
- 1 **Centurion** (use **Knight** stat block, CR 3)
- Led by the Legate if he survived Session 2

**Set-piece Combat:**
- Half the party on each side of the river
- Ranged attacks across the water
- Melee on the bridge
- Environmental hazards: fast current, slippery logs

**Moral Choice:**
- The Romans are just following orders
- Killing them makes the party murderers in Rome's eyes
- Can they find a way to stop them without bloodshed?

## Scene 4: The Sacred Grove

Deep in the forest lies an ancient Germanic holy site—older than Rome, older than memory.

**Read aloud:**
> You emerge into a clearing unlike any in the forest. Seven standing stones ring a granite altar, each carved with spiraling runes that seem to shift in the firelight. The air thrums with power—old power, wild power. The spear pulses in response, eager and hungry. Thusnelda the druid kneels at the altar: "Here was where the God-Killer was forged. Here it must be unmade."

**The Ritual Requirements:**

Thusnelda explains:
1. The spear must be placed on the altar
2. Blood of a warrior must anoint it (1d6 damage, voluntary)
3. A sacrifice must be made—something precious given willingly
4. The party must speak the words: "We unmake what was made in darkness"

**The Sacrifice:**
- Can be a magic item
- Can be a memory (lose proficiency in a skill)
- Can be an NPC ally who volunteers
- **Or** someone can take the corruption upon themselves (instant corruption level 5)

## Scene 5: Mars Intervenes

As the ritual begins, **Mars himself manifests**—not physically, but as a divine presence.

**Encounter: Avatar of Mars**
- Use **Fire Elemental** stat block (CR 5) but reskin as divine wrath
- Appears as a towering figure of flame and fury
- Speaks: "THE SPEAR IS MINE! YOU DARE DESTROY WHAT I CREATED?"

**Combat Options:**
1. **Fight:** Destroy the avatar (hard battle)
2. **Complete the Ritual:** Hold it off while one person finishes the ritual (skill challenge)
3. **Bargain:** Offer Mars something else in exchange (what could a god want?)

**Skill Challenge: Complete the Ritual**
- 4 successes before 3 failures
- Each round, one person makes a check while others defend:
  - Religion DC 15 (speak the words of unmaking)
  - Arcana DC 15 (channel the grove's ancient magic)
  - Athletics DC 13 (hold the spear steady)
  - Insight DC 14 (resist the spear's influence)

## Conclusion

**If they destroy the spear:**
- It shatters with a scream that echoes across the spiritual realm
- Mars' avatar dissipates, roaring: "YOU HAVE MADE AN ENEMY OF WAR ITSELF!"
- The corruption fades from anyone affected
- Thusnelda: "You have done what heroes of old could not. But Mars does not forget."

**If they fail or keep it:**
- Mars claims the spear—or whoever holds it
- They must flee with a god's wrath upon them

**Advance to Level 6**

## DM Notes

### Guy Sclanders' "Sacrifice Principle"
Victory should cost something meaningful. Players should feel they've earned the win through sacrifice, not just dice rolls. The ritual's sacrifice requirement embodies this perfectly.

### Branching Paths
This session has multiple valid outcomes based on player choices:
- Path A: Spear destroyed, Mars angry, party must deal with consequences
- Path B: Spear kept, corruption spreads, different finale ahead
- Path C: Bargain struck with Mars, new alliance formed

Track which path they take for Session 4's opening.
# Chapter 4: Return to Rome

## Session Overview

The party returns to Rome as either heroes or fugitives. They must navigate the political intrigue of the Senate, discover who truly wanted the spear, and prepare for the final confrontation.

**Level:** 6 → Advance to 7 at session end
**Key Goal:** Uncover the conspiracy and choose a side

## Opening: The Eternal City

**If spear was destroyed:**
> Rome rises before you like a marble dream. The Capitoline Hill gleams white in the sun, the Senate House stands proud, and the Colosseum roars with distant crowds. But beneath the beauty, you sense rot. The gods are angry. Mars most of all.

**If spear still exists:**
> Rome beckons like a serpent's smile. Somewhere in those marble halls, someone is searching for the spear you carry. Someone powerful. Someone willing to kill for it. The city that once felt like home now feels like a trap.

## Scene 1: Audience with the Senate

The party is summoned before a secret Senate committee in a private chamber.

**Present:**
- **Senator Gaius Cassius Brutus** (the primary villain)
- **Senator Marcus Tullius** (an ally)
- **Vestal Virgin Cornelia** (neutral observer)
- **Tribune Lucius** (if alive)

**Senator Brutus' Proposal:**

He's smooth, charming, and utterly ruthless:
- Congratulates them on dealing with the "frontier incident"
- Reveals he was behind the Tribune's orders
- Offers them wealth, land, citizenship for their families
- **In exchange:** They must retrieve the spear (if destroyed) or give it to him (if it exists)

**His True Plan (DC 16 Insight to sense):**
- He plans to use the spear to assassinate the Emperor during the upcoming Triumph
- Install himself as dictator "to restore the Republic"
- Believes Mars will bless this if enough blood is spilled

**Senator Tullius' Warning:**
- Pulls them aside afterward
- "Brutus is mad. He dreams of being Romulus reborn."
- Offers to help stop Brutus if they'll testify against him

## Scene 2: The City of Shadows

The party has 24 hours before the Emperor's Triumph. They can investigate:

**Option A: Follow Brutus' Agents**
- DC 14 Stealth to tail them through Rome
- Discover an underground temple to Mars
- Cultists preparing a ritual to resurrect the spear
- Combat: 6 **Cult Fanatics** (CR 2 each)

**Option B: Gather Evidence**
- DC 15 Investigation in the Senate archives
- Find records of Brutus embezzling military funds
- His correspondences mention "the God-Killer returns"
- Can use this to turn other Senators against him

**Option C: Seek Divine Aid**
- Visit the Temple of Jupiter
- The High Priest performs augury (costs 100 gp)
- Jupiter speaks through omens: "The scales must balance. Blood for blood. Ambition for humility."
- Cryptic but helpful: suggests Mars can be appeased with a worthy sacrifice

**Option D: Confront Brutus Directly**
- Dramatic scene in his villa
- He monologues about Rome's corruption
- "The Republic died with Caesar. I will resurrect it."
- Can convince him to stand down (DC 18 Persuasion) or goad him into revealing plans

## Scene 3: The Underground Temple

Following clues leads to Brutus' secret temple beneath Rome.

**Read aloud:**
> Beneath the city, deeper than the sewers, you find it—a cavern carved into living rock. The walls run red with ochre paint made to look like blood. An altar of black stone dominates the center, and upon it... a spear. Not the original—a replica, but one infused with dark ritual magic. Around it, cultists chant in Latin: "Mars Ultor! Mars Ultor! Avenge us!"

**Encounter:**
- **Senator Brutus** (use **Priest** stat block but with +2 AC from magic armor)
- 4 **Cult Fanatics**
- 1 **Gladiator** (his personal bodyguard)

**The Ritual:**

If the party disrupted it in time:
- Brutus rages: "You've doomed Rome! The gods demand blood!"
- He attacks with the replica spear (functions as +1 magic spear, but not cursed)

If they're too late:
- Brutus completes the ritual
- Mars partially possesses him (**Brutus transforms: HP doubled, +4 to hit, attacks twice per turn**)
- "I AM THE HAND OF MARS! I WILL PURGE ROME IN FIRE AND BLOOD!"

## Scene 4: The Emperor's Triumph

The climax occurs during the public Triumph—the Emperor's victory parade.

**Setting:** The streets of Rome, thousands of citizens watching

**If Brutus escaped:**
- He makes his move during the parade
- Attempts to kill the Emperor with the spear/replica
- Party must stop him publicly
- Chase scene through crowds (skill challenge)

**If Brutus was captured:**
- His remaining agents make the attempt
- Party must identify the assassin among the crowd
- DC 16 Perception each round (3 rounds before the Emperor is in danger)

**Public Combat:**
- Fighting in front of Rome
- Civilians scream and flee (difficult terrain)
- Praetorian Guards join the fight
- Every action is witnessed—reputation consequences

## Conclusion

**Victory Outcomes:**

**If they saved the Emperor:**
- Honored as heroes
- Granted Roman citizenship and land
- But: Mars is still angry about the spear

**If they exposed the conspiracy:**
- Senate is purged of conspirators
- They're celebrated but also feared
- "Those who topple Senators can topple anyone"

**If Brutus escaped:**
- He's now a fugitive with dangerous knowledge
- Sets up potential sequel
- Mars' cult grows in the shadows

**Advance to Level 7**

## DM Notes

### Senator Brutus as Villain
Using Guy Sclanders' villain archetypes, Brutus is a "Never Present" villain until this session. The party has been fighting his plans, not him directly. Now he's revealed—and he's not just evil, he's a **tragic antagonist** who genuinely believes he's saving Rome.

### Moral Complexity
Players might sympathize with Brutus' goals (ending corruption, restoring the Republic) even if they disagree with his methods. This creates dramatic tension—classic Sclanders storytelling.

### Setting Up the Finale
Whatever outcome, Session 5 will deal with Mars' wrath. The god of war doesn't forgive, and the party has made an enemy of divinity itself.
# Chapter 5: The Wrath of Mars

## Session Overview

The final confrontation. Mars, god of war, demands satisfaction for the destruction of his spear. The party must face divine judgment, make ultimate sacrifices, and determine the fate of Rome itself.

**Level:** 7 (final level)
**Key Goal:** Survive Mars' judgment and end the campaign

## Opening: The Sky Bleeds Red

Three days after the Triumph, omens appear across Rome.

**Read aloud:**
> The sky turns red at noon. Not sunset red—blood red. The Tiber runs backwards. Every dog in Rome howls in unison. In the Forum, the statue of Mars begins to weep crimson tears. The augurs declare what everyone already knows: the god of war is coming, and he is angry.
>
> That night, you all share the same dream. You stand in a vast arena of black sand beneath a blood-red sky. Mars himself sits upon a throne of swords and bones. His voice shakes your souls: "YOU DESTROYED WHAT WAS MINE. NOW PAY THE PRICE."

## Scene 1: The Divine Summons

The party awakens in a pocket dimension—Mars' personal arena, pulled from the mortal world.

**The Arena:**
- Circular, 200 feet across
- Black sand that drinks blood
- Walls lined with the weapons of fallen heroes
- No escape except victory or death

**Mars' Judgment:**

The god appears—15 feet tall, clad in bronze armor, face hidden behind a Corinthian helmet.

**His Voice (speak with gravitas):**
> "MORTALS. You defied a god. You destroyed the Spear of Broken Oaths, forged in divine fire and quenched in the blood of my son. For this crime, you will either PROVE YOUR WORTH in combat, or DIE screaming. Choose your champion—or face me as one."

**The Challenge:**
- Option A: One party member fights Mars in single combat
- Option B: Entire party fights Mars together (harder but shared risk)
- Option C: Attempt to bargain (what can you offer a god?)

## Scene 2: Trial by Combat

**Option A: Champion vs. Mars**

Mars summons a **Divine Champion** (use **Warlord** stat block, CR 12 but adjust for single PC):
- Actually a manifestation of Mars' power, not the god himself
- HP: 150, AC: 18
- Multiattack: Three attacks with spear (+9 to hit, 1d8+5 piercing + 2d6 fire)
- **Divine Fury**: Once per turn, can make an extra attack
- **War God's Resilience**: Resistance to all damage except radiant

**Arena Mechanics:**
- Weapons of fallen heroes line the walls (can grab them as bonus action)
- Each weapon grants different power:
  - **Gladius of Achilles**: +2 weapon, advantage on attack rolls but vulnerability to piercing
  - **Shield of Horatius**: +3 AC, can use reaction to impose disadvantage on attacks
  - **Spear of Leonidas**: Reach 10 ft, +1d10 damage if you haven't moved this turn

**Victory Condition:**
- Reduce the champion to 0 HP
- Or survive 10 rounds (Mars will respect endurance)

**Option B: Party vs. Mars (Himself)**

**Mars, God of War** (Custom stat block):
- HP: 300, AC: 20
- Legendary Resistance (3/day)
- **Multiattack**: Mars makes four attacks
- **God-Forged Blade**: +12 to hit, 2d8+7 slashing + 2d8 fire
- **Divine Smite**: (Recharge 5-6) All creatures within 20 feet, DC 18 Con save, 8d6 fire damage (half on success)
- **Legendary Actions** (3/turn):
  - **Attack**: Make one weapon attack
  - **War Cry**: Frightened condition, DC 16 Wisdom save
  - **Summon Warriors**: 4 Shadows of the Fallen appear (use **Specter** stats)

**Tactical Notes:**
- Mars is arrogant; exploitable with clever tactics
- The arena weapons help but have costs
- He respects courage—reckless bravery can impress him

## Scene 3: The Bargain

**If they attempt to bargain:**

Mars laughs: "WHAT COULD MORTALS OFFER A GOD?"

**Possible Offers:**
1. **Service**: Become his champions, fight in his name for a year and a day
2. **Sacrifice**: One party member offers their life willingly
3. **Victory**: Win a war in Mars' name (leads to future quest)
4. **Honor**: Promise to build a new temple to Mars and spread his glory
5. **Knowledge**: Reveal secrets of divine weapons (if they learned anything)

**Persuasion Check:**
- DC 20 Persuasion or DC 18 Religion (understanding what gods value)
- Success: Mars accepts, modifies the trial
- Failure: "YOU INSULT ME WITH EMPTY WORDS!" (combat begins)

**Compelling Offers:**
- "Great Mars, we destroyed the spear not to defy you, but because it was being misused by men unworthy of your gift."
- "Let us prove our devotion by serving you—not as slaves, but as honored champions."
- "The spear's corruption dishonored your name. We purified your reputation."

## Scene 4: The Price of Victory

**After defeating/bargaining with Mars:**

The god is impressed (or satisfied):

**Mars' Decree:**
> "You have proven yourselves worthy. Few mortals have stood before a god and lived. I grant you this: your lives, and my grudging respect. But know this—you have interfered with divine will. All actions have consequences."

**The Cost (pick 2-3 based on campaign):**

1. **Marked by War**: Each character bears Mars' brand (glowing red scar). They have advantage on Intimidation checks but disadvantage on Persuasion with peaceful folk. They are called to war whenever Mars wills it.

2. **The Burden**: One character (their choice) becomes Mars' Chosen. They gain a powerful boon (resistance to fire, extra attack, etc.) but must answer Mars' call once per year to fight in his name.

3. **The Debt**: They owe Mars a future favor—to be called in when he chooses. This could lead to a sequel campaign.

4. **The Transformation**: One willing character is transformed into a demigod servant of Mars—they leave the party to serve at his side (noble sacrifice ending for that PC).

5. **The Prophecy**: Mars reveals a dark prophecy about Rome's future: "In three generations, Rome will burn. Only those marked by war can prevent it." (Sequel hook)

## Scene 5: Return to Rome

The party awakens in the Temple of Mars in Rome, collapsed before the altar.

**Epilogue Choices (based on their decisions throughout):**

**Heroic Ending:**
- Hailed as saviors of Rome
- Granted estates, citizenship, military commissions
- Senators seek their counsel
- But they carry Mars' mark—forever changed

**Pragmatic Ending:**
- Quietly rewarded, given land on the frontier
- They leave Rome, knowing too much about its corruption
- Settle as veterans, with Mars' blessings and curses

**Tragic Ending:**
- One or more died in Mars' arena
- Survivors honored but broken
- Rome celebrates them as martyrs
- Their sacrifice is remembered in legend

**Rebellious Ending:**
- They rejected both Mars and Rome
- Now hunted, they flee beyond the Empire
- Freedom, but at the cost of home
- Legends grow of the "God-Defiers"

## Final Scene: Where Are They Now?

Have each player describe where their character is one year later:
- What are they doing?
- How has Mars' mark affected them?
- What do they remember most about this campaign?
- What rumors do they hear about the others?

**DM Final Narration:**
> The Shadow of Mars is long. What you did in those five sessions echoes across the Empire. Some call you heroes. Some call you fools. Mars himself calls you... interesting. And in the divine realm, that is far more dangerous than being forgotten. Your story is over. But legends never truly end.

## Campaign Conclusion

Thank the players. Roll credits. Share highlights.

**Experience Wrap-Up:**
- All characters end at level 7
- Award bonus XP for exceptional roleplay
- Distribute any remaining treasure/rewards

**Sequel Hooks** (if desired):
- Mars' prophecy about Rome's burning
- Brutus' cult surviving in shadows
- Other gods taking interest in the party
- Germanic alliance or conflict
- The spear's fragments scattered across the world

## DM Notes: Guy Sclanders' Principles Applied

### Master Plot
The entire campaign followed the "Spear's Journey": Discovery → Corruption → Destruction → Divine Judgment. Each session escalated the stakes while maintaining player agency.

### Theme
**Duty vs. Free Will** - The party was caught between Rome's orders, the gods' will, and their own conscience. Every major decision forced them to choose what they truly valued.

### Villain Archetypes
- **Legate Corvinus**: Blunt Force Nemesis (direct opposition, Session 1-2)
- **Senator Brutus**: Never Present (manipulating from shadows, revealed Session 4)
- **Mars**: Divine Antagonist (ultimate challenge, Session 5)

### Player Agency
Throughout, multiple paths were valid. No "right" answer—only consequences. This is peak Sclanders design.

### Meaningful Sacrifice
From the grove ritual to Mars' final price, victories cost something precious. Players should feel they *earned* their ending.
